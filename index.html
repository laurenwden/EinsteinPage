Albert Einstein

(E=mc2)

Physicist, Scientist (1879-1955)
10 Things You Didn't Know About Albert Einstein:
  • He is commonly referred to as the "people's scientist" because of his sense of humor and approachability.
  • His family believed his head was oversized.
  • At 17, he renounced his German citizenship to avoid having to go into the military.
  • He didn't speak his first words until 3 or 4 years of age. His reponse when asked why he hadn't spoken up to that point was "because up to now, everything was in order."
  • Einstein's brain can be viewed today at the Mutter Museum in Philadelphia.
  • He gave out autographs and photos for a fee and donated the money to charity.
  • There is a periodic element named after him despite him making no contribution to its discovery.
  • He was spied on by the FBI because he was believed to be a radical and a communist.
  • Einstein's life could have been longer. When offered surgery, he refused becuase he believed in dying a natural death and that not doing so was "tasteless".
  • After his death, Einstein's eyeballs were given to his doctor. Today they are stored in a safety deposit box in New York City.
